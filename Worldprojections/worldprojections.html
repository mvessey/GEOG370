WGS84 Projection

The WGS84 projection is a fairly accurate projection. Where it falls short, and where distortions begin to appear are as the latitudes move further and further away from the equator.

Aitoff Projection

This projection has issues with distortion vertically as it moves further away from the prime meridian.

Mercator Projection

This projection will accurately display the correct shapes of continents. However, sizes of the continents may become distorted as you move further away from the equator..

Robinson Projection

Overall, this map is fairly accurate, however, its distortions become more and more evident as you move further away from the equator.

Winkel l Projection

This projection does not show distances accurately. Additionally, vertical distortion can be seen in this projection.

Equal Area Cylindrical Projection

This is a cylindrical projection, thus it experiences severe distortion near the poles as the latitude increases and decreases further away from the equator.

Equidistant Conic Projection

This is a cylindrical projection, thus it experiences severe distortion near the poles as the latitude increases and decreases further away from the equator. As it is seen clearly in the map below, as you move further away from the north pole more and more distortion is present.

North Pole Azimuthal Equidistant

This is a cylindrical projection, thus it experiences severe distortion near the poles as the latitude increases and decreases further away from the equator.
